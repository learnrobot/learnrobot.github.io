The Miracle of Walking Again

A paralyzed patient walked holding his daughter's hand for the first time in 10 years. The miracle was made possible by a 'Wearable Exoskeleton Robot'.

The robot reads the minute electrical signals generated by the patient's muscles, understands the intention to walk, and moves the mechanical legs. It's the warmest technology that restores lost human abilities, rather than replacing humans.

The Furry Robot Soothing Loneliness

The seal-shaped companion robot 'PARO', designed for seniors living alone and dementia patients, entered the Guinness World Records as the world's most therapeutic robot.

When seniors who cannot keep live animals stroke the robot, it makes eye contact and responds with warmth. It has proven to be more effective at reducing depression than medication, showing that a robot can be a comforting family member rather than a cold machine.

Iron Men in the Flames

Fire scenes exceeding 1,000 degrees Celsius, and collapse sites where visibility is blocked by smoke. Quadruped robots and reconnaissance drones are the first to be deployed to these places where firefighters can hardly enter.

They locate survivors using thermal imaging cameras and clear paths by removing heavy debris. Technologies originally developed as military weapons are now serving as rescuers, saving human lives.
